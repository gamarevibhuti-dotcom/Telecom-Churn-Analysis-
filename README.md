EDA On Telcom Customer Churn Analysis

Project Overview
This project analyzes customer churn data to identify the key factors influencing customer retention and customer attrition. The analysis was performed using Python, Pandas, Matplotlib, and Seaborn, with multiple visualizations created to uncover churn patterns and business insights.

Dataset Summary
•	Total Customers Analyzed: 7,043
•	Total Features: 21
•	Missing Values: 0
•	Duplicate Customer IDs: 0
•	Average Customer Tenure: 32.37 months
•	Average Monthly Charges: $64.76
•	Average Total Charges: $2,279.73

Key Quantitative Findings
1. Overall Churn Rate
•	Approximately 26.5% of customers have churned.
•	Around 73.5% of customers have been retained.
•	This indicates that nearly 1 out of every 4 customers leaves the service, highlighting the importance of retention strategies.
2. Gender Impact on Churn
•	Churn behavior is relatively similar across male and female customers.
•	Gender does not appear to be a significant driver of customer churn.
3. Senior Citizen Analysis
•	Senior citizens represent a smaller portion of the customer base.
•	However, the percentage of churn among senior citizens is noticeably higher than among non-senior customers.
•	This suggests that senior customers may require targeted support and retention programs.
4. Tenure Analysis
•	Customers with short tenure (1–2 months) show the highest churn tendency.
•	Customers with longer tenure are significantly more likely to remain loyal.
•	Early customer engagement and onboarding initiatives could reduce churn during the first year.
5. Contract Type Analysis
•	Customers on month-to-month contracts exhibit the highest churn rates
•	Customers with one-year and two-year contracts demonstrate substantially higher retention.
•	Longer contract commitments are strongly associated with customer loyalty.
6. Internet Service Analysis
•	Customers using Fiber Optic Internet show the highest churn levels.
•	Customers using DSL services and those without internet services demonstrate lower churn.
•	This may indicate pricing, service quality, or customer expectation issues within Fiber Optic offerings.
7. Payment Method Analysis
•	Customers using Electronic Check have the highest churn rate among all payment methods.
•	Alternative payment methods show comparatively better retention performance.
•	This customer segment should be investigated further for potential billing or satisfaction issues.
8. Paperless Billing Analysis
•	Customers enrolled in Paperless Billing display higher churn levels.
•	This segment may overlap with customers on month-to-month contracts and electronic payment methods, contributing to elevated churn risk.

Business Recommendations
1.	Focus retention efforts on customers within their first year of service.
2.	Encourage migration from month-to-month plans to annual contracts through discounts and loyalty incentives.
3.	Investigate customer experience issues among Fiber Optic Internet users.
4.	Promote value-added services such as Online Security, Technical Support, and Device Protection bundles.
5.	Develop targeted retention campaigns for senior citizen customers.
6.	Analyze Electronic Check users to identify billing-related dissatisfaction.
7.	Implement proactive churn prediction models to identify at-risk customers before cancellation.

Conclusion
The analysis reveals that customer churn is primarily influenced by contract type, tenure, internet service category, payment method, and the availability of value-added services. Customers with short tenure, month-to-month contracts, Fiber Optic connections, Electronic Check payments, and fewer support services are most likely to churn. By addressing these factors, the company can significantly improve customer retention and reduce revenue loss associated with customer attrition.
